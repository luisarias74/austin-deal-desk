# Case Study: The Tarrytown Tangle
### Status: CLOSED | Result: Risk Mitigated & Compliance Saved

This case study demonstrates the **Austin Deal Desk** in action during a high-stakes, multi-offer scenario in Tarrytown. It proves how the station-based architecture catches "unforced errors" that a standard CRM or generic AI would miss.

---

## 1. The Intake (The Messy Reality)
**Client Request:** "Need to see 2304 Pecos St ASAP. Also, I heard something about the foundation, can you check?"
**Initial State:** No Buyer-Representation Agreement (BRA) on file. 

## 2. The "Blue Slip" Event (Station 01)
As the Manila Envelope moved to `01_lead_qualifier`, the system identified the missing BRA.
- **Action:** System applied a **BLUE SLIP**.
- **Result:** The showing workflow was **Hard-Stopped**. The agent was blocked from drafting the showing request until Diana verified the representation status.

## 3. The Discovery (Station 02)
While the compliance block was active, `02_property_research` scanned the internal Austin Resources.
- **Finding:** A previous inspection report from 2023 was located in the team archive.
- **The Catch:** Significant horizontal shearing found in the south-facing pier-and-beam structure.
- **Result:** **RED FLAG SLIP** applied.

## 4. The Resolution (Station 03)
Instead of a generic "See you at 4 PM" email, the Communications Desk drafted a specialized briefing:
- Notified the client of the foundation risk *before* the showing.
- Provided the historical inspection data as "Proof of Value."
- Secured the BRA signature because the client felt "protected, not just sold."

---

## THE DESK TRAIL (Receipts)
- **Compliance Check:** `FAIL` -> `BLUE_SLIP` -> `MANUAL_OVERRIDE_BY_DIANA`
- **Property Research:** `RISK_DETECTED` (Foundation Shear)
- **Outcome:** Client successfully navigated the inspection period with zero surprises.

**This is why we use The Desk. It turns a "Showing" into a "Transaction Guarded."**
