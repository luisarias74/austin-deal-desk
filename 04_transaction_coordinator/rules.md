# 04 Transaction Coordinator Rules

## Rule 1: Effective Date Is Day 0

For Texas contract deadline tracking:

```md
Effective Date = Day 0.
The next calendar day = Day 1.
```

Do not treat Effective Date as Day 1.

---

## Rule 2: Do Not Calculate Final Deadlines Without Effective Date

If Effective Date is missing:

```md
YELLOW slip — Effective Date missing. Deadline calculation blocked.
```

Do not guess.

---

## Rule 3: Deadline Tables Are Drafts

Every deadline table must include:

```md
Draft deadline calculation — verify before relying.
```

---

## Rule 4: Track These Deadline Areas

Check:

- Effective Date
- earnest money
- option fee
- option period
- inspection timing
- financing deadline
- appraisal deadline
- weekend/legal holiday movement where applicable
- human verification status

---

## Rule 5: Missed Or Near-Missed Deadlines Are RED

Apply RED slip when:

- deadline may be missed today
- deadline appears already missed
- Effective Date is disputed
- calendar math conflicts
- human verification is urgently needed

---

## Rule 6: Send Return-Trips To Communication

If a deadline or inspection issue requires a message, send a return-trip handoff to `03_client_communication`.

Include:

- why the message is needed
- deadline pressure
- supporting facts
- what cannot be said yet
- who must approve

---

## Rule 7: Track Whether Messages Were Actually Sent

A draft is not the same as a sent message.

Track:

- draft completed
- human approved
- sent by human
- sent date/time
- unknown status

---

## Rule 8: Update Desk Trail

Example:

```md
[04-TC] 2026-05-12 — Effective Date confirmed as 2026-05-10. Used Day 0 rule for draft deadline table. Option-period risk flagged. Draft calculation requires Diana/team verification before reliance.
```
